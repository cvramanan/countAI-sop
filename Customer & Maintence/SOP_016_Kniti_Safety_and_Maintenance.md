# Safety, Maintenance & Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)
## Knit-I Inspection System (Version 11 & Version 12)

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines standard safety practices, electrical guidelines, maintenance procedures, and operating responsibilities for the Knit-I Inspection System.

It ensures:
- Operator safety
- System reliability
- Accurate defect detection
- Long-term equipment performance

---

## 2. Scope

This SOP applies to:
- Operators
- Supervisors
- Maintenance Engineers
- Technical Teams

Applicable Systems:
- Knit-I Inspection System Version 11
- Knit-I Inspection System Version 12

---

# 3. SAFETY PRECAUTIONS

## 3.1 Power Safety (Mandatory Before Any Work)

### Step-by-Step Procedure:

1. Switch OFF the Knit-I machine from the main power source.
2. Turn OFF the MCB / isolator switch.
3. Confirm the display screen is fully OFF.
4. Wait a minimum of 2 minutes before opening the cabinet or touching internal components.

> ⚠ Never perform electrical or camera maintenance while power is ON.

---

# 4. ELECTRICAL MAINTENANCE GUIDELINES

## 4.1 Input Voltage Requirements

| Parameter | Required Value |
|------------|----------------|
| Input Voltage | 220V – 240V AC |
| Ground-Neutral Voltage Difference | < 2V |

---

## 4.2 Earth Voltage Check Procedure (Monthly)

### Tools Required:
- Digital Multimeter

### Steps:

1. Set the multimeter to AC voltage mode.
2. Measure voltage between:
   - Neutral and Ground
3. Confirm the reading is below 2V.
4. If above 2V:
   - Inform the electrical department immediately.
   - Do not operate the system until resolved.




---

# 5. DAILY & PERIODIC MAINTENANCE

---

# 5.1 Camera Unit Maintenance

## A. Camera Protection Rules

### DO’s
- Keep the camera unit covered when the machine is idle.
- Clean acrylic cover gently.
- Inspect lens clarity daily.

### DON’Ts
- Do NOT spray full-pressure air directly.
- Do NOT use an air gun close to the lens.
- Do NOT allow oil mist accumulation.
- Do NOT open the camera housing.

---

## B. Cleaning Camera Acrylic Cover (3–4 Times Daily)

### Tools Required:
- Soft microfiber cloth
- Approved acrylic/lens cleaner

### Step-by-Step Cleaning Procedure:

1. Turn OFF the machine.
2. Use a dry microfiber cloth first.
3. If dirt remains:
   - Lightly moisten the cloth with cleaner.
   - NEVER spray liquid directly on the acrylic.
4. Wipe gently in:
   - Circular motion OR
   - Single directional stroke.
5. Do NOT apply pressure.
6. Ensure no streak marks remain.

Regular cleaning ensures accurate defect detection.

---

# 5.2 Cabinet Maintenance

## DO’s
- Cover the cabinet during servicing of nearby machines.
- Ensure proper ventilation.
- Keep the cabinet closed at all times.
- Check cooling fans monthly.

## DON’Ts
- Do NOT keep the cabinet open unnecessarily.
- Do NOT allow dust accumulation.
- Do NOT place liquids on top of the cabinet.

---

# 5.3 Camera Wiring Guidelines

## DO’s
- Inspect cables weekly.
- Secure cables properly.
- Replace damaged cables immediately.

## DON’Ts
- Do NOT use broken cables.
- Do NOT bend LAN cables sharply.
- Do NOT pull cables forcefully.

Broken cables may cause:
- Camera disconnection
- Signal instability

---

# 6. SPECIAL OPERATING RECOMMENDATIONS

## 6.1 Tubular Mode / No Lycra Running

### Steps:

1. Unplug Camera 2 light power supply.
2. Turn OFF the “Lycra Defect” option in the UI.
3. Confirm the system is running normally.

---

# 7. FINAL CHECK AFTER MAINTENANCE

## 7.1 Connection Verification Procedure

### Step-by-Step:

1. Check HDMI cable connection.
2. Check touch cable connection.
3. Check Camera 1 & Camera 2 LAN connections.
4. Check controller connection.
5. Check internet LAN cable.

### Confirmation:

- Green blinking lights must be visible behind:
  - Hubs
  - Cameras

Blinking green indicates proper communication.

---

# 8. OPERATOR RESPONSIBILITIES

- Clean camera covers daily.
- Report electrical fluctuations.
- Monitor error messages.
- Ensure proper shutdown procedure.
- Inform maintenance team for abnormalities.

---

# 9. MAINTENANCE ENGINEER RESPONSIBILITIES

- Monthly earth voltage check.
- Quarterly cable inspection.
- Cabinet inspection.
- Cooling fan cleaning.
- Electrical connection tightening.

---

# 10. FINAL SAFETY DECLARATION

Before leaving maintenance site, confirm:

- [ ] Power connections secure
- [ ] Cabinet closed properly
- [ ] All cables tightened
- [ ] No tools left inside cabinet
- [ ] System running without error
- [ ] UI operating normally
