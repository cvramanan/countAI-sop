# STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE

## Title: Daily Stand-up Meeting Process for Software Team

**SOP ID:** SOP_002  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** 12.2.2026  
**Prepared by:** Siva  
**Approved by:** --  

---

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this SOP is to define a structured and time-bound daily stand-up meeting process for the software team to ensure:

- Clear visibility of daily progress  
- Early identification of blockers  
- Alignment on daily priorities  
- Improved accountability and communication  

---

## 2. Scope

This SOP applies to:

- Project Manager (PM)  
- Software Developers  
- QA Engineers  
- DevOps Engineers  
- UI/UX Developers (if applicable)  

---

## 3. Meeting Schedule

**Frequency:** Daily (Monday to Saturday)  

**Morning Stand-up Time:** 10:00 AM – 10:30 AM  
(If required, the meeting may be scheduled at any suitable time before 11:00 AM.)

**Duration:** Maximum 20–30 minutes  

---

## 4. Participants

- Project Manager (PM)  
- All assigned Software Team Members  

Attendance is mandatory unless prior approval is obtained.

---

## 5. Meeting Structure

The meeting shall be concise and strictly time-boxed.

Each team member must answer the following three questions:

1. What was completed yesterday?  
2. What is planned for today?  
3. Are there any blockers preventing task completion?  

Updates must be task-focused and clearly communicated.

---

## 6. Roles and Responsibilities

### 6.1 Project Manager (PM)

The PM is responsible for:

- Starting and ending the meeting on time  
- Ensuring all members provide updates  
- Keeping discussions within scope  
- Identifying and recording blockers  
- Assigning follow-up actions if required  
- Escalating critical issues when necessary  

The PM must ensure the stand-up does not turn into a detailed technical discussion. Separate meetings should be scheduled for deep discussions.

---

### 6.2 Software Team Members

Each member is responsible for:

- Joining the meeting on time  
- Providing clear and concise updates  
- Mentioning relevant task IDs (e.g., Jira ID)  
- Highlighting blockers immediately  
- Avoiding lengthy explanations  

**Example Update Format:**

Yesterday: Completed API validation logic for FIS-21  
Today: Start integration testing for FIS-22  
Blockers: Waiting for database schema approval  

---

## 7. Meeting Rules

- The meeting must start and end on time  
- No detailed technical discussions during stand-up  
- Side conversations are not allowed  
- Updates must be factual and measurable  
- Blockers must be clearly stated  
- Only relevant participants should attend  

---

## 8. Blocker Handling Process

If a blocker is identified:

1. The PM records the issue  
2. A responsible person is assigned  
3. A separate discussion is scheduled if required  
4. A target resolution timeline is defined  

Critical blockers impacting delivery must be escalated immediately.

---

## 9. Evening Stand-up (If Required)

An evening stand-up may be conducted in the following cases:

- Tight release deadlines  
- Critical production issues  
- Multiple blockers affecting progress  

**Suggested Time:** 5:30 PM – 6:00 PM  

Purpose: Quick review of daily progress and confirmation of issue resolution status.

---

## 10. Documentation

Stand-up summaries may include:

- Completed tasks  
- Ongoing tasks  
- Identified blockers  
- Risks impacting delivery  

Documentation can be maintained in:

- Jira  
- Project tracking sheet  
- Internal status tracker  

---

## 11. Review and Revision

This SOP shall be reviewed periodically or when:

- Team size changes  
- Project structure changes  
- Process improvements are introduced
