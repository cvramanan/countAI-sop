# Internal Team Communication SOP
**Role:** Technical Support Engineer  
**Department:** Technical Support  
**Version:** 1.0  

---

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this SOP is to define a standard, clear, and traceable communication process between the Technical Support team and internal teams such as Software, Hardware, DevOps, and Management.

This ensures faster issue resolution, accountability, and reduced miscommunication.

---

## 2. Scope
This SOP applies to:

- Communication related to support tickets
- Software, hardware, and infrastructure issues
- Escalations, updates, and resolution confirmations

This SOP does not cover customer-facing communication.

---

## 3. Internal Teams Involved
- Technical Support (L1)
- Software Engineering Team (L2)
- Hardware Engineering Team (L2)
- DevOps / Infrastructure Team
- Engineering Manager / Support Lead

---

## 4. Communication Channels
Approved internal communication channels:

- Ticketing system (primary source of truth)
- Official email
- Approved collaboration tools (Jira, Microsoft Teams)

**Note:** Informal conversations must always be documented in the ticket.

---

## 5. Communication Principles
All internal communication must be:

- Clear and structured
- Professional and respectful
- Relevant and factual
- Documented and traceable

Avoid assumptions or unverified information.

---

## 6. Ticket-Based Communication (Mandatory)

### 6.1 Ticket Updates
- All technical findings must be updated in the ticket
- Use clear technical descriptions
- Attach logs, screenshots, or diagnostics

### 6.2 Ownership
- L1 Support remains the ticket owner
- L2 teams provide investigation and fix details
- Support team communicates final resolution

---

## 7. Communication During Escalation

### 7.1 Escalation Message Must Include
- Ticket ID
- Issue summary
- Impact and priority
- Troubleshooting steps already performed
- Required action from the receiving team

### 7.2 Escalation Etiquette
- Do not escalate without analysis
- Avoid tagging individuals unnecessarily
- Follow defined escalation hierarchy

---

## 8. Cross-Team Collaboration

### 8.1 Software Team Communication
- Share reproducible steps
- Provide logs and error traces
- Confirm environment details

### 8.2 Hardware Team Communication
- Share device ID, serial number
- Diagnostic results
- Physical inspection findings

### 8.3 Validation Team Communication
- Share device ID, serial number
- Diagnostic results

---

## 9. Status Updates & Follow-Ups
- Follow up within SLA if no response
- Update ticket after each interaction
- Avoid repeated pings without context

---

## 10. Priority & Critical Issue Communication

### 10.1 P1 / Critical Issues
- Immediate ticket update
- Notify team via approved channel
- Inform Support Lead / Manager

### 10.2 Daily / Weekly Updates
- Share issue summaries if required
- Highlight blockers and dependencies

---

## 11. Meeting & Call Communication
- Schedule calls only when necessary
- Document discussion outcomes
- Update action items in the ticket

---

## 12. Information Security & Confidentiality
- Share sensitive data only with authorized teams
- Follow access control policies
- Avoid sharing customer data unnecessarily

---

## 13. Prohibited Practices
- Resolving issues without ticket updates
- Using unofficial communication tools
- Sharing partial or misleading information
- Publicly blaming teams or individuals

---

## 14. Review & Continuous Improvement
- Review internal communication issues regularly
- Improve escalation clarity
- Update SOP as processes evolve
