# Customer Communication SOP
**Role:** Technical Support Engineer  
**Department:** Technical Support  
**Version:** 1.0  

---

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this SOP is to establish a standard and professional communication process with customers throughout the support lifecycle.

Effective communication ensures transparency, trust, and customer satisfaction while issues are being investigated and resolved.

---

## 2. Scope
This SOP applies to:

- All customer-facing communication
- Issue acknowledgment, updates, escalation notifications, and closure communication

This SOP applies to communication via:

- Ticketing system
- Email
- Approved communication tools (as defined by the company)

---

## 3. Communication Principles
All customer communication must follow these principles:

- Clear and professional language
- Polite and respectful tone
- Accurate and honest information
- Timely updates based on SLA
- No internal or sensitive information shared

---

## 4. Communication Channels
Approved channels for customer communication:

- Support ticket comments
- Official support email
- Customer portal

**Note:** Verbal or chat communication must be documented in the ticket.

---

## 5. Initial Issue Acknowledgment

### 5.1 Timeline
- Acknowledge customer issues within defined SLA

### 5.2 Acknowledgment Content
Include:

- Confirmation that the issue is received
- Ticket reference number
- Next steps and expected response time

**Example:**
> “We have received your request and created ticket ID #12345.  
> Our team is currently reviewing the issue and will update you shortly.”

---

## 6. Ongoing Status Updates

### 6.1 Update Frequency

| Priority | Update Frequency |
|----------|------------------|
| P1 | Every 1–2 hours |
| P2 | Every 4 hours |
| P3 | Daily |
| P4 | As required |

### 6.2 Update Content
Each update must include:

- Current status
- Actions taken
- Next steps
- Expected timeline (if available)

Avoid vague updates such as:

- “We are checking”
- “Issue is in progress”

---

## 7. Communication During Escalation
When an issue is escalated:

- Inform the customer clearly
- Do not mention internal team names or individuals
- Provide realistic expectations

**Example:**
> “The issue has been escalated to our engineering team for further investigation.  
> We will keep you updated on the progress.”

---

## 8. Handling Delays and SLA Risks
If resolution is delayed:

- Inform the customer proactively
- Explain the reason in simple terms
- Share revised timelines

**Example:**
> “The issue requires additional investigation, and resolution may take longer than initially expected.  
> We appreciate your patience.”

---

## 9. Handling Customer Frustration or Escalation Requests
- Remain calm and professional
- Acknowledge customer concern
- Avoid defensive responses
- Escalate internally if required

**Example:**
> “We understand the inconvenience caused and are actively working to resolve this as quickly as possible.”

---

## 10. Information Security & Compliance
- Do not share internal logs, code, or confidential details
- Do not share other customer information
- Follow company data protection policies

---

## 11. Resolution & Closure Communication

### 11.1 Resolution Confirmation
Before closing the ticket:

- Confirm issue resolution with the customer
- Provide a clear summary of the fix

**Example:**
> “The issue has been resolved by applying the latest update.  
> Please confirm if everything is working as expected.”

### 11.2 Ticket Closure Notification
- Inform the customer before closing
- Provide reopening instructions

**Example:**
> “We will proceed with closing this ticket.  
> If the issue reoccurs, please reopen or create a new request.”

---

## 12. Communication Documentation
- All customer communication must be logged in the ticket
- Phone or chat discussions must be summarized in writing

---

## 13. Prohibited Communication Practices
- Sharing unverified information
- Making commitments without confirmation
- Using informal or unprofessional language
- Blaming internal teams

---

## 14. Review & Continuous Improvement
- Review customer feedback regularly
- Improve templates and communication standards
- Update SOP as needed

---

## ✅ Outcome of This SOP
- Consistent and professional customer communication
- Improved customer trust and satisfaction
- Reduced misunderstandings and escalations
