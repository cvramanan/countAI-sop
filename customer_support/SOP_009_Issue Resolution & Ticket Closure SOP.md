# Issue Resolution & Ticket Closure SOP
**Role:** Technical Support Engineer  
**Department:** Technical Support  
**Version:** 1.0  

---

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this SOP is to define a standardized process for validating issue resolution and closing support tickets in a consistent and controlled manner.

This ensures customer satisfaction, accurate documentation, SLA compliance, and knowledge retention.

---

## 2. Scope
This SOP applies to:

- All support tickets handled by the Technical Support team
- Software, hardware, configuration, and user-related issues
- Customer-reported and internal tickets

This SOP covers activities from resolution confirmation to final ticket closure.

---

## 3. Roles & Responsibilities

### Technical Support Engineer (L1)
- Validate the issue resolution
- Communicate resolution to the customer
- Update ticket details accurately
- Close the ticket as per process

### Software / Hardware Teams (L2)
- Provide fix, workaround, or replacement details
- Confirm technical resolution

### Support Lead / Manager
- Review critical (P1/P2) ticket closures
- Ensure SOP and SLA compliance

---

## 4. Resolution Validation Process

### 4.1 Technical Validation
Before closing a ticket, the Technical Support Engineer must:

- Confirm the fix has been applied successfully
- Test the solution (where applicable)
- Ensure no related or follow-up issues exist

### 4.2 Customer Validation
- Inform the customer that the issue is resolved
- Request confirmation from the customer

**Example:**
> “The reported issue has been resolved. Please verify and confirm if the system is functioning as expected.”

---

## 5. Resolution Documentation Requirements
Each resolved ticket must include the following information:

- Root cause of the issue
- Resolution steps performed
- Fix or workaround details
- Team involved (Support / Software / Hardware)
- Date and time of resolution

❌ Avoid unclear comments such as:
- “Issue fixed”
- “Working now”

---

## 6. Ticket Closure Procedure

### Step 1: Update Ticket Status
- Set the ticket status to **Resolved** or **Pending Customer Confirmation**

### Step 2: Add Resolution Notes
- Clearly describe what was done to resolve the issue
- Mention software version updates, configuration changes, or hardware replacement details

### Step 3: Attach Supporting Evidence
- Logs, screenshots, test results, or diagnostic reports (if applicable)

### Step 4: Notify Customer
- Inform the customer about ticket closure
- Provide instructions to reopen the ticket if required

---

## 7. Closure Timelines (Customer Confirmation)

| Priority | Wait Time Before Closure |
|----------|--------------------------|
| P1 | Same business day |
| P2 | 1 business day |
| P3 | 2 business days |
| P4 | 3 business days |

If no response is received:
- Send a reminder
- Close the ticket with proper documentation

---

## 8. Auto-Closure Conditions
A ticket may be closed automatically when:

- Customer confirms resolution
- No response is received after follow-up reminders
- Monitoring alerts confirm system stability

---

## 9. Post-Closure Activities

### 9.1 Knowledge Base Update
- Create or update knowledge base articles for recurring issues

### 9.2 Root Cause Analysis (RCA)
- Perform RCA for P1 issues and frequently recurring problems
- Share findings with relevant teams

---

## 10. Reopening Tickets
- Tickets can be reopened if the same issue reoccurs
- Reopened tickets must reference the original ticket ID

---

## 11. Quality Standards
- All mandatory ticket fields must be completed
- Resolution notes must be clear and professional
- Ticket history must be audit-ready

---

## 12. Prohibited Closure Practices
- Closing tickets without customer notification
- Closing tickets without validating resolution
- Closing tickets only to meet SLA without proper checks

---

## 13. Exceptions
- Emergency fixes may be closed after validation and manager approval
- System-generated alerts may close automatically after stability confirmation

---

## 14. Review & Continuous Improvement
- Review closed tickets periodically
- Identify gaps and improvement areas
- Update this SOP as required

---

## ✅ Outcome of This SOP
- Consistent and accurate ticket closure
- Improved customer satisfaction
- Better reporting and knowledge sharing
